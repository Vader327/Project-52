Origami Designs

Today, many people are attracted to the idea of learning how to fold origami figures because paper is a cheap craft supply. When origami was first practiced, however, it was a craft only for the elite. Japanese monks folded origami figures for religious purposes. As paper became more affordable, common people began making origami figures as gifts or creating folded cards and envelopes for their correspondence. Origami also started to be used as an educational tool, since the folding process involves many concepts that are relevant to the study of mathematics.

Camel


  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go!

Chameleon


  • Almost half of all known chameleon species live in madagascar.
  • Chameleons vary wildly in terms of size.
  • Chameleons mainly change their color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Pigeon


  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.

Teddy Bear


  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  • A teddy has been into space! It boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.
  • "Teddy Bear" was one of the first illustrated books based on these popular soft toys, written by Alice Scott.

Panda


  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.
  • Pandas are "lazy" - eating and sleeping make their day.

Cicada


  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
